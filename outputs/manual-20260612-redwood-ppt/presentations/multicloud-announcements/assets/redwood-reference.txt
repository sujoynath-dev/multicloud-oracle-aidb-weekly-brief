Press F6 to navigate to the most recent message region.\nSkip to home\nDesign for Oracle Redwood\nWhy a design system?\n\nTens of thousands of Oracle developers are together creating thousands of user interfaces for tens of millions of end users. Creating state-of-the-art quality at that scale requires careful collaboration and coordination, a shared vision around high-end quality, and lots of patience and discipline. The Oracle Redwood Design System is the product of that careful collaboration and a shared vision, which is expressed in high-fidelity patterns, templates, and components. This means that we can quickly create highly sophisticated and polished interactions, and when we make improvements, we can make them in one component and have the benefits accrue to tens of millions of users all at once.\n\nLearn more about the Oracle Redwood Philosophy\n\nStart designing Oracle Redwood applications\n1. Understand the problem\n\nUse the Clarity of Purpose framework to identify your users, write user goals, and describe the shape of data of your use case. Gather feedback from Product Management and other stakeholders, and iterate until you achieve clarity.\n\n2. Articulate your vision\n\nDefine your ideal UX by documenting your common scenarios. Choose scenarios that your users will commonly have rather than edge cases. Once you and your stakeholders have agreed on the scenarios in words, translate the words into core task flows and user journeys. This part of the process should define the visionary design and should not be constrained by near-term deadlines.\n\n3. Identify Oracle Redwood page templates to use\n\nStart with the Oracle Redwood Pattern Book to translate your core flows into an Oracle Redwood experience. The Oracle Redwood Pattern Book shows how Oracle Redwood page templates support specific user goals and have different layouts and configurations to achieve multiple UI variations. By starting with page templates, your users will have a coherent experience across Oracle Redwood applications, and you’ll be able to design and build your products faster and easier. In addition, as the design system evolves, your designs and code and customers’ extensions will be upgrade-safe.\n\n4. Detail and refine your design\n\nUse Figma to create UI wireframes. Work with the existing configurations and capabilities of the different page templates and components in the design system, using the largest building blocks possible. Starting with the largest building blocks means less hand-coding by the development team. While designing, stay focused on the problem you are solving. Design with a minimal UI approach: a simple solution is better than an over-designed UI that delivers more than what is needed.\n\nTo learn more about using the toolkit for your prototypes, see Redwood Design System Toolkit.\n\n5. Specify your design for development\n\nMap your design to the existing page templates and components, and validate your plan with your development team. Verify your understanding of how the components work and revise your wireframes and specifications as needed. Clarify component behaviors in the component demos and use the design forums as necessary.\n\n6. Monitor engagement in the real world\n\nOnce you've developed the first phase of your experience on the way to delivering the full vision, take the opportunity to learn from real users using your design in their everyday tasks. You can gather data from telemetry and field visits to understand where your design is resonating, and where there are opportunities for improvement. With that knowledge, you can continue developing your experience toward your vision, making appropriate adjustments based on real-world customer observations.\n\nYou found the goody bag!\n\nWe've cooked up a fresh set of digital Redwood Design System goodies for you to enjoy.
